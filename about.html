About the instructor

A teacher first, in two subjects at once.

Rook and Reader is one person: a parent of four who has spent years teaching, writing, and playing chess long before any of it became a business.

Background

An MFA in fiction, on top of a bachelor's degree in English, sits behind the literacy program. That training led to university level teaching, freelance journalism, book reviews, and short fiction, along with a period teaching at a Florida public high school. Every reading list built for a student draws on that same background, the same standard applied for years to curating what his own children read.

Chess, in parallel

Chess has run alongside the writing life the whole way through. He currently teaches a school chess class of first and second graders and has organized a complete class tournament from the ground up: pairings, standings, and custom certificates for every student. That same structure is what shapes the chess program here.

Outside the classroom

Home, garden, and family

He and his wife live in Lake County with their four children, all of whom attend private school. She works full time as a nurse; he runs the household day to day, cooking, teaching chess, and managing the home, alongside this program.

At home

Cooking

Cooking is one of the ways he shows care for his family, and it shows up in how he approaches teaching too: attention to the person in front of him, not a fixed recipe applied to everyone the same way.

Outside

Gardening

An active home gardener growing vegetables and fruit trees using an organic, chemical free approach, the same patience and long view that shapes how a reading list or a chess curriculum is built over time.

With his kids

Reading aloud

He has hand selected nearly every book his own children have read, including reading the Earthsea novels aloud from start to finish, a habit of trusting good books to do the work.

A straightforward note

Where things stand

He is not currently working from an active Florida teaching certificate; instruction here is built on an MFA, university teaching experience, and years of hands on classroom and tournament experience instead. He's glad to talk through background and approach directly with any family before a student starts.
