Approach

Reading like a writer, from the start

Comprehension is the floor, not the ceiling. Students read real literature — chosen deliberately for craft and age — and are taught to notice how it's built: structure, voice, and the choices an author made on purpose. Writing instruction grows directly out of that reading, rather than sitting beside it as an unrelated worksheet.

Reading

A curated, individual reading list per student — not a generic grade-level list. Selections are matched to reading level, interest, and what a student is ready to be challenged by next, drawn from a personally built home library spanning early readers through YA and classic literature.

Writing

Composition instruction grounded in real craft: narrative structure, voice, revision, and clarity — the same fundamentals taught at the university level, scaled down and made concrete for younger writers.

How it works

What each student actually gets

  • 01

    A starting conversation

    Every student begins with a short assessment of where they read and write today — not a placement test, a real conversation about what they enjoy and what's hard.

  • 02

    A built reading list

    A sequence of titles selected specifically for that student, updated as they grow — the same standard applied to hand-curating a personal home library for years.

  • 03

    A working lesson plan

    Sessions built around what the student is currently reading, pairing discussion of the text with matched writing exercises — not disconnected grammar drills.

  • 04

    Ongoing adjustment

    Reading lists and lesson plans are revisited regularly. A student who's ready to move faster, or needs to slow down, gets a plan that actually reflects that.
