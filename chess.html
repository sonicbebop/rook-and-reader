Chess · Grades K–8

From learning the moves to playing in a real tournament.

Most local chess options stop at teaching the rules. This one doesn't — students progress toward genuine competitive play, taught by an instructor who currently runs a school chess class and has organized a full scholastic tournament from the ground up.

Approach

A real progression, not a single workshop

Chess enrichment nearby tends to be a single recurring session with no clear next step. Here, students move through a defined sequence — fundamentals, tactics, opening principles, and tournament preparation — grouped by age and level rather than crammed into one mixed classroom.

Small, level-banded groups

Grouped roughly K–2, 3–5, and 6–8, so beginners aren't lost and stronger players aren't bored — something a single homeschool co-op class rarely has the numbers to offer.

Built by someone who runs tournaments

Beyond weekly instruction, students get exposure to real competitive structure — standings, pairings, and the format of an actual tournament — drawn directly from experience organizing one.

The path

Where a student goes, in order

  • 01

    Fundamentals

    Piece movement, rules, and basic checkmates — taught for retention, not just so a student can technically play a legal game.

  • 02

    Tactics & pattern recognition

    Forks, pins, skewers, and the tactical patterns that decide most games at this level — drilled until a student starts seeing them, not just naming them.

  • 03

    Opening principles

    Not memorized lines, but the reasoning behind sound openings — development, center control, king safety — so students can think through unfamiliar positions.

  • 04

    Tournament preparation

    Clock management, notation, tournament etiquette, and live practice games under real conditions, building toward participation in organized scholastic events.
